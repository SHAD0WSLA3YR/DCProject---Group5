Temporal lobe: Processes auditory information and is involved in memory and emotion.

Frontal lobe: Responsible for higher cognitive functions such as decision making, problem-solving, and voluntary movement.

Parietal lobe: Processes sensory information such as touch, temperature, and pain, and is involved in spatial awareness and perception.

Occipital lobe: Primarily responsible for visual processing.

Cerebrum: Largest part of the brain, responsible for higher brain functions such as thinking, perceiving, and planning.

Brain stem: Controls basic life functions such as breathing, heart rate, and consciousness.
